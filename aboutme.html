About Me

The story behind SheBuilds — and why I built it for you.

Hi, I'm Niya Emery — a student, builder, and advocate for women in tech. My path into IT wasn't a straight line. I started college as a nursing major, then pivoted to cybersecurity at St. John's University, and now I'm studying Information Technology at NJIT. Every detour taught me something I couldn't have learned any other way.

I built SheBuilds because I wished something like it existed when I was starting out. There were so many moments where I felt like I was the only one figuring things out from scratch — no roadmap, no community, just a lot of googling at midnight.

This site is everything I would have wanted: a career quiz, a learning roadmap, real history about women who shaped computing, and a place to connect with others walking the same path.

My journey

How I got here.

It took a few wrong turns to find the right direction — but looking back, every step made sense.

  • Year 1
    Started as a Nursing Major

    I thought I wanted to help people through healthcare. I learned quickly that I wanted to help people — just differently.

  • Pivot
    Discovered Cybersecurity — St. John's University

    A single elective course changed everything. I was hooked on how cybersecurity sat at the intersection of logic, creativity, and real-world impact.

  • Now

    Studying Information Technology with a focus on web development and network systems. Building SheBuilds as my class project — and hopefully something that lasts beyond the semester.

  • Next
    Whatever comes next

    Internships, certifications, and growing this community. The path is still being written.
